OBSERVIUM·AI

A model, a reference architecture, and a protocol for integrated transdisciplinary observability — detecting compound risks that emerge between institutional and disciplinary boundaries, not within any single one.

🧩 Systems Model 🏗️ Reference Architecture 📐 Recommended Protocol

Background

The crisis lives in the gaps between institutions

A farmer is bitten by a cobra near a paddy field just before harvest. The health centre, if it reports at all, records a snakebite admission. The species — a cobra, near a field, at monsoon onset, 200 metres from a forest edge cleared six months ago for construction — is never recorded. The ecological signal is lost. The agricultural signal is lost. The pattern connecting this bite to seven others in the same zone over three weeks is invisible to every agency individually.

The forest department doesn't know. The agricultural officer doesn't know. The panchayat lead doesn't know. The researcher whose species-distribution model predicted exactly this risk zone doesn't know. This is not a data-collection failure — it is an architectural one. The systems that exist were designed to answer single questions within single mandates. None was designed to ask what one incident means across every domain it touches at once.

⚠️ What this costs
India loses an estimated 58,000 people to snakebite every year. 94% of those deaths are rural; 77% happen outside health facilities. Official surveillance captures roughly 10% of expected hospital deaths — before counting the majority that never reach a facility at all. The ecological and agricultural signals that predict this risk with ~76% accuracy already exist. What's missing is a protocol that connects them at the moment a bite occurs.

A model, not a product

OBSERVIUM·AI v1.0 is a reference architecture and protocol specification — not a platform, application, dashboard, or product. The closest analogy is UML: a language for describing how systems should be built, independent of any codebase. Any cross-domain monitoring deployment — a district snakebite system, a marine observatory, an urban wildlife-conflict tracker — can be built on this framework regardless of its technology stack. The framework defines what should happen, in what order, with what guarantees. The implementation is yours.

Snakebite in Karnataka is the grounding scenario, not the subject. A leopard in a peri-urban area, an elephant in a crop corridor, a water-contamination point — each is one incident across four institutional mandates, none of which owns the whole picture. OBSERVIUM·AI was built for all of them.

Architecture

A nine-node signal ontology across three pillars

Every input begins as a raw Signal and, through defined transformation steps, becomes Intelligence that reaches the right decision-maker at the right time. Each step has a name, a rule, and a purpose. Nothing is discarded; everything is preserved — which is what lets the system answer, in 2026, questions about something captured in 2024.

Signal Ontology v1.0 — pipeline
Signal ─▶ Incident ─▶ Typed Incident ─▶ Event ─▶ Compound ─▶ Reaction ─▶ Intelligence ─▶ Outcome
  1         2              3              4         5            6             7              8
  │                                                                                          │
  └──────────────────────────── feedback: outcomes recalibrate detection ◀───────────────────┘
                         Exception Branch (9) — escape route for signals that don't fit, attachable at any node
  • Signal → Incident → Typed Incident: raw input is preserved, given a permanent trace ID, then classified across every domain it belongs to simultaneously (a snakebite is a health and an ecology incident in the same pass).
  • Event: multiple typed incidents from the same scene bind into one richer, redundant record — if the voice report fails, the photo persists.
  • Compound: a cross-domain pattern that no single agency sees — health cases + camera-trap anomalies + an antivenom stock gap + monsoon onset, bound by spatial, temporal, and systemic-factor dimensions.
  • Reaction → Intelligence → Outcome: a pattern crossing threshold fires a new signal, an alarm, and a consequence cascade; the Intelligence layer presents ranked options (AI presents, humans decide); outcomes feed back and recalibrate the system.

The three pillars

🟢 Frontline Capture

Nodes 1–4. Any input, any modality, any observer — voice, image, sensor, text, and Indigenous & Local Knowledge (ILK) as a first-class modality. Offline-first store-and-forward, raw preservation, five-level redundancy.

🟠 Midline Synthesis

Nodes 5–6. Where cross-domain intelligence is created: three-dimensional Compound binding, a configurable Systemic Factor Registry, cross-domain deduplication, escalation algebra, and Risk Window Mode.

🔴 Backline Decision-Making

Nodes 7–8. Five-layer stakeholder routing, RAG-based intelligence synthesis, span-level governance (SHACL over JSON-LD), outcome tracking, and the adaptive calibration feed.

Protocol

What a compliant instantiation must do

OBSERVIUM·AI defines the what; your implementation is the how. A compliant v1.0 instantiation must:

  • Implement all nine nodes of the Signal Ontology with the defined transformation rules.
  • Preserve raw inputs at Node 1 alongside every structured field added later.
  • Assign trace_id / span_id at Node 2 and propagate them through every node (end-to-end causal reconstruction).
  • Apply multi-domain simultaneous classification at Node 3.
  • Implement three-dimensional binding at Node 5 with a configurable Systemic Factor Registry.
  • Implement the five-layer stakeholder routing topology at Node 7.
  • Enforce span-level governance via SHACL over JSON-LD.
  • Implement the feedback loop from Node 8 back to Node 1.

The governance boundary — hardcoded vs configurable

🔒 Hardcoded (non-overridable)
  • IHR 2005 notification obligations (Annex 2 criteria)
  • CBD Article 26 biodiversity reporting
  • Sendai Framework disaster-risk thresholds
  • National wildlife-act notification requirements
  • Hardcoded routing fallback paths
⚙️ Configurable (per deployment)
  • Domain taxonomy & Systemic Factor Registry
  • Spatial / temporal binding windows
  • Escalation thresholds & domain-count multipliers
  • Stakeholder routing paths (Layers 3–4)
  • ILK credibility scoring, outcome indicators, Risk Window criteria
Five-layer stakeholder routing topology
5 · Broadcast      Risk Window Mode, emergency & public channels
4 · Informal       monitoring roles, community bulletins, guest authority   ┐ defined +
3 · Configurable   organisation-defined escalation paths                    ┘ discovered
2 · Regulatory     IHR · CBD · Sendai · wildlife acts
1 · Hardcoded      non-negotiable — always fires, cannot be overridden
Interoperability principle
Agencies don't need a shared mandate to coordinate — they need a shared identifier and a delivery contract. The common trace ID and the routing topology supply both. Integration with existing systems (GBIF Darwin Core, WHO IDSR/IDSP, Camtrap DP, iNaturalist, ODK) is via endpoint-relay adaptors — no agency has to change its infrastructure.

Features

What the framework guarantees
🎙️ Multimodal capture

Voice, image, video, sensor, text, and ILK — from any device, in any language, online or offline.

📶 Offline-first redundancy

Store-and-forward queues and five-level redundancy: no incident is lost to network failure.

🏷️ Multi-domain typing

One classification pass tags an incident across every domain it belongs to — transdisciplinarity at the data level.

⚗️ Compound formation

Cross-domain patterns bound by spatial, temporal, and systemic-factor dimensions surface what silos miss.

🗺️ Stakeholder topology

Five routing layers with defined and discovered stakeholders, plus RACI for the response.

🤝 ILK as first-class data

Indigenous & Local Knowledge enters as an equal input under CARE Principles and Local Contexts TK Labels.

🔐 Span-level governance

Governance attaches to processing spans, not flat records — deterministic, auditable, and portable with the data.

🕰️ Retroactive queryability

Raw preservation means old incidents can be re-analysed against new models and evidence published after capture.

🔄 Learning loop

Intervention outcomes recalibrate detection thresholds and binding rules — the deployment gets more accurate over time.

AI as an operational nervous regulator
AI accelerates structuring (Node 2), classification (Node 3), pattern recognition (Node 5), and intelligence synthesis (Node 7) — but it does not make hardcoded/regulatory routing decisions, does not activate discovered stakeholders without human confirmation, and does not decide interventions. The framework functions without AI; AI makes it precise, responsive, and scalable.
